Emperor Penguin

Overview

The Emperor Penguin (Aptenodytes forsteri) is the largest of all penguin species and is well adapted to life in Antarctica.

Physical Characteristics

  • Height: 1.1 to 1.3 meters (3.6 to 4.3 feet)
  • Weight: 22 to 45 kg (49 to 99 lbs)
  • Appearance: Black head, back, and tail; white belly; yellow-orange markings on the neck and upper chest

Habitat & Distribution

Emperor Penguins are found exclusively in Antarctica, especially on the sea ice surrounding the continent.

Diet

They mainly eat fish, squid, and krill. They can dive up to 500 meters (1,640 feet) and stay underwater for around 20 minutes to hunt.

Reproduction & Parenting

Breeding starts in winter. Males keep the egg warm in their brood pouch for about 65 days while fasting. Once the chick hatches, both parents take turns feeding it.

Survival Adaptations

  • Thick layers of fat and feathers to withstand extreme cold
  • Huddle together in large groups to conserve heat
  • Can slow metabolism and redirect blood flow to survive long fasting periods

Conservation Status

Listed as Near Threatened due to climate change and loss of stable sea ice.
